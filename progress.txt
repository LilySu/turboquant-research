# TurboQuant Research & Implementation Progress Log

## Project Context

This project implements TurboQuant, a near-optimal vector quantization algorithm for KV cache compression in LLMs. The goal is to:

1. Deeply understand the theoretical foundations (Shannon, Lloyd-Max, QJL)
2. Implement TurboQuant in CUDA with production-grade efficiency
3. Integrate with GLM5 and DeepSeekV3.2
4. Validate against paper benchmarks

## US-002: Research Beta Distribution on Hyperspheres
Date: 2026-03-28

### What was done:
- Created research/02-hypersphere-distributions.md (1368 words, LaTeX-formatted)
- Prerequisite refreshers: orthogonal matrices, hypersphere visualization ($d=2,3$), Gamma function
- Derived Lemma 1 via surface area slicing in 4 steps
- Showed convergence to $\mathcal{N}(0, 1/d)$ with heuristic proof and numerical comparison
- Covered concentration of measure with sub-Gaussian tail bound

### Key learnings:
- The derivation is geometric: marginal density = cross-sectional surface area / total surface area
- Verification for $d=3$ gives uniform on $[-1,1]$ — Archimedes' hat-box theorem
- Covariance between coordinates is $-1/(d(d-1)) \to 0$, justifying near-independence
- Variance is exactly $1/d$ by symmetry: $\sum x_j^2 = 1 \Rightarrow d \cdot \mathbb{E}[x_j^2] = 1$

### Files created/modified:
- research/02-hypersphere-distributions.md (new)
- prd.json (US-002 passes: true)
- progress.txt (this entry)

### Mathematical insights:
- Lemma 1: $f_X(x) = \frac{\Gamma(d/2)}{\sqrt{\pi}\,\Gamma((d-1)/2)}(1-x^2)^{(d-3)/2}$
- Convergence: $\log(1-x^2) \approx -x^2$ gives Gaussian shape with variance $1/d$
- Sub-Gaussian tail: $\mathbb{P}(|x_j| > t) \leq 2\exp(-dt^2/2)$
