# TurboQuant Research & Implementation Progress Log

## Project Context

This project implements TurboQuant, a near-optimal vector quantization algorithm for KV cache compression in LLMs. The goal is to:

1. Deeply understand the theoretical foundations (Shannon, Lloyd-Max, QJL)
2. Implement TurboQuant in CUDA with production-grade efficiency
3. Integrate with GLM5 and DeepSeekV3.2
4. Validate against paper benchmarks

## US-001: Research Shannon's Source Coding Theory
Date: 2026-03-28

### What was done:
- Created comprehensive research document at research/01-shannon-source-coding.md (1356 words)
- Covered differential entropy h(x), mutual information I(x;y), distortion-rate function D(R)
- Documented Shannon Lower Bound (Lemma 2 from TurboQuant paper) with full formula
- Included 3 worked examples with numerical calculations
- Connected each concept to its role in TurboQuant's near-optimality proof

### Key learnings:
- TurboQuant's near-optimality factor is √3π/2 ≈ 2.72, constant across all bit-widths
- The Shannon Lower Bound is applied via Yao's minimax principle to the uniform distribution on S^{d-1}
- The SLB formula for vectors: D(p_X, B) ≥ (d/2πe) · 2^((2/d)(h(x)-B))
- Gaussian sources are the "hardest" to compress — this is why rotated coordinates (near-Gaussian) are a natural fit
